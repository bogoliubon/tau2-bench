Online Refine Comparison: Human vs LLM Seed Trajectories (Retail)

Question: Does the quality of seed trajectories matter for online policy learning?

Setup:
- 41 retail training tasks where both human (Tudor) and LLM rollouts succeeded
- 10 of those tasks (seed=42) used for batch_refine initial policies (batch_size=2)
- Online refine runs on remaining 31 train + 40 test tasks, same order for both arms
- Policy refined every 5 tasks (refine_batch_size=5)

Two arms:
- Human arm: initial policy from Tudor's human trajectories
- LLM arm: initial policy from LLM rollout trajectories

Models:
- Agent: gpt-4o
- User simulator: gpt-4.1
- Batch refine (initial policy): gpt-4o
- Online refine (policy updates): gpt-4o

Files:
- Filtered trajectories: experiments/retail/human_tudor_10_common.json, llm_rollouts_10_common.json
- Initial policies: initial_policy_human_10.json, initial_policy_llm_10.json
- Online refine logs: online_refine_human_seed.json, online_refine_llm_seed.json

Future experiments:
1. Different refine LLM: swap gpt-4o for Claude or gpt-5 for the online refinement step
2. Different N for initial policy: try fewer or more than 10 seed trajectories
3. Different seed: vary the random seed to check robustness of results
4. Task-flow-aware sampling: instead of random 10, pick 1 trajectory per task flow
   (exchange, modify, return, general inquiry — 4 flows) so the initial policy has
   coverage of every flow type. Try N=4 with one per flow, see if broader coverage
   beats random sampling
